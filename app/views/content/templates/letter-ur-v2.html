What you need to do next

You need to discuss the agreed support with your manager unless you are self-employed. If the agreed items are not already bought, your employer (or you if self-employed) can go ahead and buy them.

Important Information

By claiming against this award you are accepting the terms and conditions below:

  • you can only use your Access to Work award for the support we tell you about in this letter
  • you may only claim for the level of support that
    o you have actually received and
    o has been agreed in your award
  • if you spend your award on items not agreed by Access to Work, we will not reimburse the money spent
  • you must tell Access to Work about any changes to your circumstances as they happen. This includes changes to your personal details, disability or health condition and employment
  • you understand that DWP will carry out reviews of your funding. If your needs change between reviews, please contact Access to Work
  • you must complete the claim form in full and sign the declaration before returning it. DWP will return any incorrect or incomplete forms

Ask for the Access to Work Reconsideration Team.
If you contact us after 4 weeks, we may not be able to help you.

When we make our decisions we consider

Additional costs – we help with costs where these are over and above those a non-disabled person would require to do their job.

Meeting minimum needs – we provide support that allows individuals to overcome workplace barriers that could be caused by their disability.

Above and beyond – we provide support that is above and beyond a reasonable adjustment. This does not replace an employer’s legal duty to make reasonable adjustments for you.

Value for money – we provide support that meets your minimum needs in the most cost effective way for taxpayers.
